Summer Drinks That Belong on Your Wedding Menu

Royal Shaadi Editorial Team · June 16, 2026 · 7 min read
A summer wedding is, above all else, a test of hospitality — and nothing communicates "we thought of you" quite like a drink that's cold, beautiful, and perfectly suited to the season. Whether you're planning an intimate rooftop reception in Delhi or a sprawling garden celebration in Jaipur, the drinks on your menu deserve as much attention as the flowers on your mandap.
01

Rose & Lychee Sherbet

Welcome Drink & Signature Station

A chilled rose and lychee sherbet sets the tone before a single vow is spoken.

Welcome Drink Mocktail Floral Alcohol-Free

The rooh afza sherbet of childhood weddings has had a spectacular glow-up. In 2026, Indian couples are reimagining the classic welcome drink as a moment of intention — a first impression that reflects the wedding's entire aesthetic before guests have even found their seats. Rose and lychee sherbet threads that needle beautifully: it's rooted in a flavour most Indians know and love, elevated by the delicate perfume of fresh lychee and real rose water, and it photographs like a dream in its blush-pink avatar.

Served in tall slender glasses with a dried rose petal floated on top and crushed ice right to the brim, this drink works equally well as a non-alcoholic option for all guests and as a base for those who'd like a splash of sparkling wine. For outdoor ceremonies in the peak of May or June, ice-cold sherbet handed to guests as they arrive is not just thoughtful — it's genuinely necessary.

💡

Serving Tip: Prepare a rose syrup concentrate at least 48 hours ahead and infuse it with whole cardamom pods. The flavour deepens dramatically when given time to rest. Mix with chilled sparkling water and lychee pulp only at the time of service for maximum freshness and fizz.

02

Raw Mango Panna Cooler

Signature Summer Mocktail

Aam Panna Indian Classics Summer Special Heat-Buster

Aam panna is summer in a glass — tart, cooling, faintly smoky from the roasted raw mango, and lit with cumin and black salt. At a wedding, it carries the additional emotional weight of nostalgia: it is the flavour that reminds virtually every Indian guest of home, of childhood summers, of a grandmother's kitchen. Serving it at your wedding is an act of warmth that no imported cocktail can replicate.

The 2026 wedding version elevates the original without overcomplicating it. A roasted raw mango base is blended with fresh mint, a pinch of black salt, roasted cumin, and just enough jaggery to balance the tartness. It's served over crushed ice in generous mason jars with a mint sprig and a slim wedge of dried mango on the rim. The smokiness from the char on the mango skin is essential — skip the shortcut of mango powder and roast your mangoes properly over an open flame or in a tandoor oven for the real thing.

"Aam panna at a wedding doesn't just cool guests down — it transports them somewhere familiar and beloved."
💡

Bartender's Note: For guests who prefer a spirit, aam panna pairs strikingly well with white rum or vodka. Ask your bartender to create a "Mango Sour" option on your menu using the same base — it becomes an instant favourite at the bar and requires no extra prep work.

03

Hibiscus & Ginger Sparkling Cooler

Bar Menu & Visual Statement

Hibiscus coolers are the most visually striking non-alcoholic option on any summer wedding menu.

Hibiscus Floral Drinks Visual Drama Zero Proof

If you need one drink on your menu that will stop guests mid-sentence to ask "what is that?", hibiscus is your answer. Dried hibiscus flowers (known as gudhal in Hindi) brew into a tea of extraordinary depth — deep crimson red, sharply tangy, and faintly floral in a way that's entirely its own. Combined with fresh ginger juice and topped with sparkling water, it becomes something close to magical: a drink that looks like a fine wine in the glass, behaves like a sophisticated cocktail, and is completely alcohol-free.

For the bar menu, the hibiscus cooler can be served still or sparkling, and both versions benefit enormously from a float of fresh lime juice just before serving. The colour alone makes it indispensable for wedding photography — a tray of these passing through golden-hour light is one of those images that ends up in every highlight reel. It also happens to be genuinely good for you, rich in antioxidants, and deeply refreshing in heat.

Prep Tip: Brew a large batch of concentrated hibiscus tea (4x strength) the night before the wedding and refrigerate it. At service, dilute with equal parts sparkling water and add ice. This keeps the prep simple on the day without compromising on flavour.

04

Thandai-Inspired Milk Punch

Reception Bar & Dessert Pairing

Thandai milk punch bridges the gap between a festive wedding drink and a light dessert course.

Thandai Indian Heritage Dessert Drink Signature Bar

Thandai is technically a Holi drink, but its magnificent combination of chilled milk, rose, cardamom, saffron, fennel, black pepper, and ground nuts belongs on every summer celebration menu — and certainly on a wedding bar. The modern thandai milk punch takes this tradition and refines it: the spice blend is more finely calibrated, the milk is lighter (often a combination of full-fat and oat milk for a silkier texture), and a touch of condensed milk is swapped for a cleaner sweetener like honey or date syrup.

Served in small stemless wine glasses or old-fashioned tumblers over a single large ice sphere, it looks stunning and tastes even better. It is the rare wedding drink that works beautifully as a palate cleanser between courses, as an accompaniment to a mithai dessert spread, or simply as a satisfying closing drink for guests who've had enough spirits. For couples who want an "experience," a live thandai station with a bartender grinding spices fresh can become one of the night's most memorable entertainment points.

💡

Flavour Tip: The quality of your thandai depends entirely on freshly ground spices. Pre-made mixes exist but the difference is immediately noticeable. Grind your fennel seeds, cardamom, black pepper, and poppy seeds fresh the morning of the wedding — the aromatic difference is extraordinary and your guests will notice.

05

Nimbu Pani Elevated: The Citrus Bar

Interactive Bar Experience

Nimbu Pani Interactive Bar Customisable Guest Favourite

Every Indian summer traces back to a glass of nimbu pani. The beauty of offering it at a wedding is not just the flavour — it's the universality. No guest will ever turn down nimbu pani. The challenge and opportunity for wedding planners in 2026 is how to present this beloved classic in a way that feels special, intentional, and genuinely beautiful rather than an afterthought from the standard banquet menu.

The answer that's gaining traction is the dedicated citrus bar: a styled station where guests can customise their own glass of nimbu pani from a curated selection of options. Choices might include still or sparkling water, regular salt or kala namak, a dash of cumin water or mint syrup, and add-ins like jaljeera concentrate, a wedge of blood orange, or a sprig of fresh lemongrass. The interaction becomes entertainment. Guests spend a moment at the station, make their choices, and end up with something that feels entirely personal. It is one of those touches that costs a fraction of an open bar but generates far more conversation.

"The best wedding drinks aren't the most expensive — they're the ones that make guests feel like someone truly thought about them."
💡

Station Design Tip: Invest in beautiful glassware for your citrus bar — tall ribbed glasses or cut-crystal tumblers make an enormous difference to how even a simple nimbu pani looks and photographs. Add a small printed card describing each customisation option; guests love the context, and it prevents a queue forming while people decide.

06

Coconut & Pandan Cooler

Destination Wedding & Sundowner Bar

Coconut coolers served at the sundowner hour are becoming a staple of South Indian and destination beach weddings.

Coconut Destination Wedding Sundowner South India

For destination weddings in Goa, Kerala, or coastal Tamil Nadu, fresh coconut water is not just a drink — it is the landscape itself arriving in a glass. In 2026, beach and resort weddings are elevating this natural cooler with the addition of pandan (screwpine leaves), whose vanilla-adjacent fragrance makes coconut water taste even more exotic and beautiful. The combination is earthy and aromatic in a way that immediately evokes a lush, tropical celebration.

Served directly in young coconuts with a long paper straw and a fresh pandan leaf twisted into a flower shape, this drink is as much a décor element as a beverage. For the sundowner hour — that golden 45 minutes before the main reception begins when guests mill about and the light is extraordinary — coconut pandan coolers circulated on trays by waitstaff create exactly the kind of relaxed, festive atmosphere that no formal bar service can quite match. Add a splash of gin or white rum for those who want it, and you have an elegant cocktail without any complexity.

💡

Sustainability Tip: Young coconut shells make beautiful zero-waste vessels. Once the wedding is done, they can be composted or used as planters. Work with your caterer to source coconuts locally — in coastal regions, this will typically mean fruit that was harvested the same morning, which makes a significant flavour difference.

07

The Signature Couple's Cocktail

Bar Menu Centrepiece

A signature couple's cocktail is the most personal element your bar menu can have — and guests always remember it.

Signature Cocktail Couples Bar Personalised Bar Menu

Perhaps no trend in 2026 wedding planning has more unanimous approval than the signature couple's cocktail. It appears on the bar menu under a name the couple chooses — typically something meaningful to their relationship, the city they met in, or an inside joke only their closest friends will fully appreciate. It is served exclusively at their wedding. And it tells a story in a single sip.

For summer, the signature cocktail should always be refreshing first and complex second. A passion fruit and basil smash, a tamarind margarita, or a mango chilli sour all work beautifully in the heat while still carrying the sophistication expected of a premium wedding bar. The mocktail version of the same drink — using the same base fruits, florals, and garnish — should be prepared with equal care and served in identical glassware. Nothing creates more goodwill at a bar than a guest who doesn't drink alcohol receiving a mocktail in the same beautiful glass as everyone else, garnished just as thoughtfully.

Planning Tip: Schedule a tasting session with your bartender or caterer at least 6 weeks before your wedding. Bring a list of your favourite flavours, a fruit you love, and any spirit preference. A good bartender will work backwards from these to design something that genuinely tastes like you — not a generic template.
