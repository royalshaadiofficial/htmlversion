Grand Bollywood & Celebrity Weddings 2024: Decoding the Looks

The lehengas, the jewellery, the palettes — the year's biggest wedding style moments, broken down.

✦ By Royal Saadi Editorial ✦ December 20, 2024 ✦ 12 min read

2024 was, by any measure, one of the biggest years for big Indian weddings — a string of high-profile celebrations that dominated timelines, style pages, and group chats for months on end. Beyond the headlines, though, what these weddings really did was reset the mood board for Indian bridal fashion. Heavier silhouettes came back. Jewellery got bolder and more heirloom-inspired. Colour palettes moved away from the expected. In this guide, we break down exactly what defined celebrity wedding style this year — and how to translate each trend into a look that's genuinely wearable, and budget-friendly, for your own wedding.

The Big Picture

Why 2024 Was Bollywood's Biggest Wedding Year

Between multi-day celebrations, international guest lists, and a steady drip of never-ending outfit reveals, 2024's celebrity weddings turned Indian bridal fashion into a genuine cultural moment. Designers who usually work quietly for private clients suddenly had their work dissected outfit-by-outfit online, and regional textile traditions — Banarasi weaves, Bengal's sholapith work, South Indian temple jewellery — got a level of mainstream attention they rarely receive.

The net effect for everyday brides and grooms has been a widened vocabulary. "I want something like the recent celebrity weddings" now means something far more specific than it did a few years ago — heavier dupattas, structured cuts, and jewellery that looks like it was pulled out of a family vault rather than a showroom.

Scale, reimagined: 2024's marquee weddings turned regional craftsmanship into mainstream style inspiration.

✦ Royal Saadi Insight

Most of what trended in 2024 wasn't new invention — it was a revival. Traditional techniques (zardozi, gota patti, temple jewellery) simply got a fresh, high-visibility platform. That's good news for anyone planning a wedding: these are time-tested crafts with real local artisans behind them, not fleeting fads.

✦
Silhouettes

The Comeback of Traditional Grandeur

After a few years of minimalist, pastel-heavy bridal fashion, 2024's big weddings pulled hard in the opposite direction: rich fabrics, structured cuts, and maximalist embroidery. The lightweight, barely-there lehenga gave way to something with real weight and presence.

The structured lehenga: a stiffened, heavily embroidered silhouette that holds its shape beautifully on camera.

Most Requested

The Structured Zardozi Lehenga

Heavy metallic thread work on a stiffened silhouette that holds its shape on camera. Popularised by several big celebrity ceremonies this year, it's now the single most-requested reference in bridal ateliers.

Regional Revival

Banarasi & Kanjeevaram Drapes

Heirloom-style saree draping made a strong return for reception looks, often paired with a contrast blouse and a statement border — a nod to weaving traditions from Varanasi and Tamil Nadu.

Modern Twist

The Cape-Lehenga & Pre-Draped Sarees

For sangeet and cocktail functions, capes and pre-stitched drapes offered the drama of a traditional silhouette with far easier movement for dancing.

Understated Luxury

Ivory, Champagne & Tissue Fabrics

A quieter counter-trend: brides opting for pale, light-catching fabrics with minimal colour but maximum texture — proof that "grand" doesn't always mean "bright."

✦ Recreate It

You don't need a couture price tag to get the structured-lehenga look. Ask your tailor for a canvas or buckram lining in the skirt panels — this is what gives the silhouette its shape and is a relatively small addition to any custom order.

✦
Colour Stories

The Colour Palettes That Defined 2024 Weddings

Red is, and probably always will be, the default bridal colour in India — but 2024's celebrity weddings pushed several alternate palettes into the mainstream conversation.

The 2024 palette shift: deeper, richer tones over the pale pastels of recent seasons.

Deep Wine & Maroon

A richer, more sophisticated alternative to classic bridal red.

Champagne Gold

Popular for reception looks that photograph beautifully at golden hour.

Ivory & Pearl White

A minimalist counterpoint, often paired with gold jewellery for contrast.

Emerald & Bottle Green

A strong sangeet-night colour, especially paired with antique gold jewellery.

Dusty Rose & Blush

Softer mehndi and haldi-function shades that still photograph as festive.

Marigold & Mustard

The reigning haldi-ceremony palette, unchanged for years and still a favourite.

The common thread across all of these: richer, deeper tones photograph better under warm indoor lighting and flash photography than the pale pastels that trended a few seasons ago — a genuinely practical reason behind the shift, beyond aesthetics alone.

✦
Jewellery

Bridal Jewellery: Kundan, Polki & the Heirloom Revival

If one category defined 2024's biggest weddings more than any other, it was jewellery. Layered necklaces, uncut-diamond polki sets, and temple-style gold pieces replaced the single delicate necklace that had been trending for a few years prior.

What Was Everywhere

  • Polki & Kundan sets — uncut diamond and gold-foil jewellery, often passed down through generations, worn as the primary bridal set rather than a single statement piece.
  • Layered necklaces — a choker, a mid-length piece, and a long haar worn together rather than a single necklace.
  • Statement maang tikkas and jhoomar — headpieces made a strong return, especially for the main ceremony look.
  • Temple jewellery — South Indian gold work with deity and floral motifs, worn well beyond South Indian weddings this year.
  • Coloured gemstones — emeralds and rubies set into gold, worn as a deliberate contrast against a neutral-toned outfit.
✦ Recreate It

Renting a heavy bridal jewellery set for the main ceremony — and mixing in one or two real heirloom pieces from family — gives you the layered, "vault" look at a fraction of buying an entire new set outright. Many jewellers now offer short-term bridal rentals specifically for this reason.

✦
Menswear

The Groom's Wardrobe Gets a Glow-Up

Grooms were not an afterthought in 2024's biggest weddings — if anything, menswear got more editorial attention than in previous years. The plain bandhgala gave way to something with far more texture and detail.

Menswear, elevated: hand-embroidered fabric and a contrast safa replacing the plain matching set.

Trends Worth Noting

  • Hand-embroidered sherwanis with resham and zari work replacing plain silk options.
  • Contrast safas (turbans) — a bold-coloured turban against a muted sherwani, rather than a matching set.
  • Nehru jackets with statement buttons for sangeet and reception functions — a lighter, easier-to-dance-in alternative to a full sherwani.
  • Brooches and layered malas — grooms accessorising almost as much as brides, with heirloom brooches and layered necklaces of their own.
  • Colour-coordinated (not identical) outfits — couples choosing complementary rather than matching tones for a more editorial, less "costume" look.
✦ Recreate It

A well-fitted, quality-fabric sherwani in a solid colour with one standout element — an embroidered stole, a statement safa, or a brooch — reads as considered and current without needing head-to-toe embellishment.

✦
Pre-Wedding Functions

Mehndi & Sangeet Look Trends

Pre-wedding functions used to be treated as the "easy" outfits — this year, they got almost as much planning as the main ceremony look.

The mehndi function, dressed up: colour-matched floral jewellery instead of plain white.

Mehndi

Floral Jewellery

Fresh flower necklaces and maang tikkas, often colour-matched to the outfit rather than plain white.

Haldi

Sunshine Yellow, Reimagined

Structured yellow lehengas and pastel florals replacing the plain kurta-and-flower-jewellery look of past years.

Sangeet

Fringe, Sequins & Capes

Outfits built specifically for movement — dancing-friendly silhouettes with high drama under stage lighting.

Cocktail

Indo-Western Gowns

Draped gowns with Indian embroidery techniques — a genuine fusion category rather than a Western dress with a bindi.

Main Ceremony

Red, Reinvented

Still red, but with unexpected accents — a green dupatta, gold detailing on a maroon base, rather than pure crimson.

Reception

The Statement Saree

A single show-stopping saree, often in a fabric or drape style tied to family heritage, over a second lehenga change.

✦
Beauty

Hair, Makeup & the "Your Face, But Better" Movement

Bridal makeup in 2024 moved firmly away from the heavy, full-glam look that dominated the last decade. The dominant request in bridal makeup studios this year was some version of "natural, but done properly" — dewy skin, softly defined eyes, and a bold lip or a bold eye, but rarely both at once.

Dewy over matte: skin-like, glowing bases replaced full heavy coverage this year.

What Changed

  • Dewy, skin-like base makeup replacing full matte coverage.
  • Soft, romantic updos and braided styles over the tightly-set curls that were popular previously.
  • Statement eyes with a nude lip, or a bold lip with minimal eye makeup — rarely both maximal at once.
  • Trial-based, personalised looks — brides bringing references but asking artists to adapt them to their own features rather than replicate them exactly.
✦ Recreate It

Book at least two trials with your makeup artist before the wedding, and bring reference images primarily for inspiration on technique (draping, eye shape, lip tone) rather than expecting an exact copy — the same look reads very differently on different face shapes and skin tones.

✦
The Bigger Picture

The Couple's Look, Together

One quieter but consistent trend across 2024's big weddings: real thought given to how the couple's outfits work as a pair in photographs — coordinated palettes without being identical, and jewellery and fabric choices considered as a joint aesthetic rather than two separate outfits.

Coordinated, not matched: a shared colour family with contrasting textures and jewellery reads far more intentional than an identical outfit set.

"The best-dressed couples this year weren't the ones who spent the most — they were the ones whose bride and groom looked like they'd actually planned their outfits together."

✦
Décor Inspiration

Décor & Venue Cues Worth Borrowing

Big celebrity weddings this year leaned into florals at a scale most couples can't replicate — but the underlying design choices scale down surprisingly well.

Single-flower drama: one flower type in varying shades reads as more considered than a mixed bouquet.

  • Monochrome floral installations — a single flower type in varying shades, rather than a mixed bouquet look, reads as more expensive at any budget.
  • Fabric-first mandaps — heavy draping with a lighter floral accent, rather than an entirely flower-covered structure.
  • Warm, low lighting over bright white light for every function, including daytime ones — this alone changes how photographs feel.
  • A defined colour story per function — décor, outfit, and even food styling loosely coordinated to a single palette per event.
✦ Recreate It

Ask your florist to quote a single-flower installation (all marigold, or all roses in two tones) before you ask for a mixed arrangement — single-variety florals are often more affordable per stem and read as more considered.

✦
The Practical Bit

How to Recreate These Looks on a Real Budget

Celebrity wedding budgets are, obviously, not the norm — but the visual language they popularised can be recreated at almost any budget with the right substitutions.

Element The 2024 Trend Budget-Friendly Way to Get It
Bridal Lehenga Couture zardozi, custom fit Rent a designer-style piece; alter blouse only
Jewellery Layered polki & kundan sets Rent the main set; mix in one heirloom piece
Groom's Sherwani Hand-embroidered, custom Solid-colour rental + one statement stole
Makeup Celebrity artist, dewy natural glam Local artist + 2 trials with clear references
Décor Large-scale monochrome florals Single-flower installation at smaller scale
Overall Effect Editorial, magazine-level polish Achievable with smart substitutions

Final Details

Accessories, Footwear & Finishing Touches

The finishing details separated a good bridal look from a great one this year.

The finishing layer: juttis, a considered clutch, and asymmetric bangles complete the look.

  • Embroidered mojaris or juttis matched to the outfit, rather than plain sandals hidden under the lehenga.
  • A statement dupatta drape — pinned with a brooch or floral detail rather than left loose.
  • A single decorative hair accessory — a jadau hairpin or floral gajra — over a fully accessorised hairstyle.
  • A well-chosen clutch in a metallic or contrast tone, small enough to look intentional rather than practical.
  • Bangles stacked on one side only — an asymmetric styling choice that reads as more editorial than symmetric bangles on both wrists.
✦
The Takeaway

Key Style Takeaways for 2025 Brides & Grooms

Strip away the scale and the price tags, and 2024's biggest weddings offer a genuinely useful style brief: lean into traditional craftsmanship over trend-chasing, treat jewellery as a considered layered story rather than a single piece, give the groom's outfit as much thought as the bride's, and let the couple's looks work together as a pair rather than in isolation.

What actually lasts: a considered look, not a trend-chased one.

None of that requires a celebrity budget — it requires intention. And that, more than anything else, is what came through in this year's most talked-about wedding looks.

"Trends fade. What stays memorable is a look that felt considered — and that's true whether the budget is ₹5 lakh or ₹50 crore."
