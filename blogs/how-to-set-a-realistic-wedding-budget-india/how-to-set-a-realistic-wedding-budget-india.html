Wedding Planning

How to Set a Realistic Wedding Budget in India

✦ Royal Shaadi Editorial Team · June 2026 · 14 min read
Planning an Indian wedding without a budget is like planning a cross-country road trip without a map — thrilling for exactly thirty minutes, then quietly terrifying. The average Indian wedding now costs between ₹15 lakh and ₹1.5 crore depending on city, guest count, and ambition. This guide will walk you through every category, every hidden cost, and every smart decision that separates couples who feel in control from those who don't.
01

Understand Your True Budget Capacity

Financial Planning Foundation

The honest budget conversation is the most important one you'll have before booking a single vendor.

Financial Planning Budget Basics Family Contribution

Before you open a single venue brochure or speak to one caterer, sit down — just the two of you, and then with your families — and have a completely honest financial conversation. This is the single most important step in the entire wedding planning process, and it is the one most couples rush past in their excitement.

Your total wedding budget has three potential sources: your own savings, family contributions from both sides, and any credit or loans you're willing to take on. Map out each of these clearly. Then subtract a mandatory 15–20% buffer that you will not touch under any circumstances — this is your emergency reserve for cost overruns, last-minute additions, and the unexpected surprises that every wedding produces.

"The number that remains after subtracting your buffer is your actual working budget. Design your entire wedding around this number — not the original one."

Critically: establish who is contributing what — and whether those contributions come with strings attached. Parents who contribute financially sometimes believe they have proportional say in guest lists, ceremonies, or décor. Clarity here prevents resentment later. A polite but direct conversation now is far better than a painful argument during the wedding planning period.

1
List every source of funding
Personal savings, parents' contribution (bride's side and groom's side separately), any family gifts already received, and any credit you're willing to carry post-wedding.
2
Subtract your 20% buffer immediately
Do this before any planning. If your combined budget is ₹30 lakh, your working budget is ₹24 lakh. The ₹6 lakh buffer is untouchable until after the wedding.
3
Agree on your top 3 non-negotiables
Photography? Venue? Bridal outfit? Decide as a couple which three things you refuse to compromise on. These get allocated first; everything else gets the remainder.
4
Document everything in writing
A shared Google Sheet with all contributions, allocations, vendor payments, and deadlines. Both partners and key family members should have access to the same master document.
💡

Smart Start: Use a dedicated bank account exclusively for wedding funds. Every contribution goes in, every vendor payment goes out from this single account. You'll always know exactly where you stand — no mental accounting, no confusion.

03

Venue — Your Single Biggest Decision

Wedding Venues & Costs

From heritage havelis to 5-star ballrooms — venue type dramatically affects not just cost, but every other vendor decision you make.

Venue Booking Banquet Halls Heritage Venues 5-Star Hotels

Your venue is not just the backdrop for your wedding — it is the foundational decision that determines almost every other planning choice you make. The venue sets the ceiling for your guest count, dictates your décor possibilities, constrains which caterers you can use, and in many cases includes services (or exclusions) that dramatically affect your total spend.

In 2026, Indian couples have more venue types to choose from than ever: 5-star hotel ballrooms, standalone banquet halls, farmhouses and resort properties, rooftop venues in urban settings, heritage havelis and palaces, and outdoor lawn spaces. Each comes with a completely different pricing structure.

Venue Type City Approx. Cost (300 Guests)

5-Star Hotel Ballroom Tier 2 City ₹4L – ₹10L
Heritage Haveli / Palace Rajasthan / Jaipur ₹6L – ₹40L+ (all-inclusive)
Standalone Banquet Hall Metro ₹2L – ₹6L
Farmhouse / Resort Outskirts (all cities) ₹1.5L – ₹5L
Community Hall / Temple Ground All cities ₹30K – ₹1.5L

What most couples miss when comparing venues is the concept of included vs. excluded services. A 5-star hotel at ₹15 lakh might include catering, basic setup, and security — making it comparable in total cost to a standalone banquet hall at ₹3 lakh where you must source everything separately. Always get a complete inclusions list before making any comparison.

⚠️

Hidden Venue Costs to ask about: Generator backup charges, security deposits (often 25–50% of total), mandatory valet parking fees, corkage charges if you bring your own beverages, early-entry fees for décor setup, and GST (18% on many venue services). These can add 20–30% to the listed venue price.

💡

Negotiation Window: Venues are significantly more negotiable during off-season months (May–August and November before the peak season). Booking a Sunday morning slot instead of Saturday evening can reduce venue costs by 25–40% at the same property. Midweek dates for pre-wedding events (mehendi, haldi) can save substantially.

04

Catering — Feed Guests Well, Not Excessively

Food & Beverage Planning

Food is what your guests will talk about for years. Prioritise quality over sheer quantity of dishes.

Catering Per-Plate Cost Menu Planning Food Waste

Catering is where many Indian weddings experience the most significant cost overruns — and the most wastage. The cultural pressure to offer a lavish spread can lead couples to pay for 400 dishes when 200 guests will actually eat 80. Smart catering decisions are about quality over quantity.

In 2026, per-plate catering costs for an Indian wedding vary dramatically by city and tier. In Delhi and Mumbai, a mid-tier multi-cuisine spread runs between ₹900 and ₹1,800 per plate. In Bangalore and Hyderabad, similar quality ranges from ₹700 to ₹1,400. Rajasthan and smaller cities can deliver comparable food at ₹500 to ₹1,000 per plate.

40%

3 menus
The ideal number: separate menus for ceremony, cocktail hour, and dinner

One of the most effective ways to reduce catering costs without diminishing the experience is to order for 85% of your guest count rather than 100%. Years of data show that at least 10–15% of confirmed guests do not attend on the day. Additionally, consider live stations (chaat, pasta, taco) over buffet spreads — they create theatre, reduce waste, and often cost less per head than a full multi-course buffet.

💡

The Tasting Rule: Never finalise a caterer without a paid tasting. Pay ₹2,000–5,000 for a formal tasting session with your fiancé and key family members. Evaluate taste, presentation, temperature control, and the caterer's responsiveness to feedback. Your wedding guests will judge your wedding party primarily on the food — this is worth the investment.

  • Get at least three caterer quotes for identical menus to compare accurately.
  • Confirm whether GST (18%) is included in the quoted price or added on top.
  • Ask about their food handling team-to-guest ratio (1:25 is the minimum standard).
  • Clarify overtime charges if your event runs past the agreed closing time.
  • Discuss leftover food donation policies — many caterers now partner with NGOs.
  • Confirm backup generator arrangements to protect cold food storage.
05

Photography & Videography — The Memory Makers

Wedding Photography Investment

Your photographs and film will outlive every flower, every outfit, and every dish served at your wedding. Do not under-invest here.

Wedding Photography Videography Candid Photos Drone Coverage

Of all the things you spend money on for your wedding, photography and videography are the only ones that remain with you for decades. The flowers wilt, the food is eaten, the outfits are stored away — but the photographs and film are what you and your children and grandchildren will return to for the rest of your lives. This category deserves serious investment, and cutting corners here is one of the most commonly regretted wedding decisions.

In India's photography market, pricing has stratified clearly into four tiers. Budget photographers (₹50,000 – ₹1.2 lakh) deliver technically competent coverage but limited creative direction. Mid-tier photographers (₹1.5L – ₹4L) offer strong portfolios, consistent editing styles, and typically cover 2–3 events in a package. Premium photographers (₹5L – ₹15L) are true artists with signature styles, extensive pre-wedding consultation, and meticulous post-production. Destination wedding specialists and celebrity photographers command ₹15 lakh and above.

"Your wedding photographs are the only investment you make for your wedding that appreciates in emotional value every year."
Photography Package What's Included Price Range
Basic (1 event) 1 photographer, 500–800 edited photos, digital delivery ₹50K – ₹1.2L
Standard (2–3 events) 2 photographers, 1,500+ photos, highlight video, album option ₹1.5L – ₹4L
Premium (Full wedding) Lead + second shooter, drone, cinematic film, premium album ₹5L – ₹10L
Luxury / Destination Artistic direction, travel, multi-day, international delivery ₹10L – ₹25L+
💡

Vetting Tip: When reviewing a photographer's portfolio, look specifically at lighting quality in indoor evening shots (the hardest conditions to shoot in at Indian receptions), their ability to capture candid emotional moments, and the consistency of their colour grading across a full gallery — not just the 30 best images they've curated.

06

Bridal Outfits & Jewellery

Bridal Fashion & Styling Budget

Bridal outfit costs vary enormously — from ₹40,000 to ₹5 crore. Know what drives the price before you shop.

Bridal Lehenga Jewellery Designer Wear Groom Sherwani

Bridal outfit and jewellery costs in India have the widest range of any wedding category — from a carefully curated ₹50,000 look that photographs beautifully to a ₹50-lakh designer lehenga worn by Bollywood celebrity brides. For most couples planning a realistic budget, the outfit category needs to be approached with clear priorities before any boutique is visited.

The biggest cost driver in bridal lehengas is the embroidery technique and fabric quality. A hand-embroidered zardozi or resham lehenga from a reputable designer runs ₹1.5L – ₹8L. A well-crafted ready-to-wear lehenga from brands like Kalki, Mohey, or Sabyasachi's diffusion line can deliver 80% of the visual impact at a fraction of the cost (₹40,000 – ₹1.5L). The difference in photographs is far smaller than the difference in price.

Jewellery deserves its own budget line because it is often the most significant single expenditure in the entire bridal category. Real gold and polki jewellery for a traditional Indian bride can run from ₹5L to ₹25L or more. Many modern brides are making smart choices here: heavy gold pieces for the main ceremony, high-quality kundan or pearl sets for other functions, and rented or heirloom pieces where possible. The key insight is that jewellery photographs similarly regardless of whether the gold is real or high-quality imitation — so reserve real gold for sentimental heirloom value, not visual impact.

💡

The Rental Revolution: High-quality designer bridal lehenga rentals have exploded in 2026. Services like Flyrobe, Stage3, and Rent It Bae offer designer labels for 10–20% of purchase price. For your mehendi and sangeet outfits — which you'll wear once — rental makes extraordinary financial sense. Save the purchase budget for your wedding day look, which often has heirloom significance.

07

The Hidden Costs Nobody Warns You About

Budget Overrun Prevention

Hidden Costs GST Service Charges Gratuity

Every couple who has planned an Indian wedding will tell you the same thing in hindsight: the budget they planned for was not the budget they ended up spending — and the gap was filled by costs they never anticipated. Here is the comprehensive list of hidden and easily overlooked costs that derail wedding budgets across India.

"The GST shock alone has left couples short by ₹3–5 lakh. Build it into every vendor quote from day one."
  • GST (18%) on most services: Photography, décor, entertainment, wedding planning services — confirm whether quotes are exclusive or inclusive of GST. A ₹3 lakh photography package becomes ₹3.54 lakh with tax.
  • Service charges at 5-star venues: Most premium hotel venues add 10–12% service charges on top of the listed price. Read the fine print on every contract.
  • Pre-wedding event costs: Mehendi, haldi, sangeet, and engagement — these are real events with real costs. Many couples plan meticulously for the wedding and wing the rest, leading to 4 events eating a budget planned for 2.
  • Hair and makeup for family members: Your mother, mother-in-law, sisters, and bridesmaids will also need professional hair and makeup. Budget ₹3,000–8,000 per person per event across 4–6 people.
  • Alterations and fittings: Even a ready-to-wear outfit will need professional alterations. Budget ₹8,000–25,000 per outfit for quality tailoring work.
  • Vendor gratuity / tips: It is customary and appreciated to tip your vendors at the end of a successful wedding. Budget ₹500–2,000 per vendor (photographer, caterer leads, décor team, DJ, etc.).
  • Travel and accommodation for out-of-town family: If you're asking family to come from another city, budget at least partially for their stay.
  • Post-wedding album and prints: Premium photographers often charge separately for physical albums (₹25,000–80,000) and this can be a surprise cost after the wedding.
  • Last-minute add-ons: Every wedding has them — a floral upgrade, a lighting addition, an extra sound system. Keep ₹50,000 – ₹1 lakh specifically for these inevitable impulse decisions.
  • Honeymoon costs: Often forgotten in wedding budget planning but typically ₹1L–5L for a domestic or international trip. Budget this separately but plan it simultaneously.
⚠️

Contract Clause Alert: Always check your vendor contracts for cancellation and postponement clauses. In 2026, most reputable vendors require 25–50% non-refundable advance deposits. Read every clause before signing — and photograph all signed agreements for your records.

08

Smart Savings Without Sacrificing Experience

Budget Optimisation Strategies

The most memorable weddings are built on experiences, not on excess — intimacy always wins over spectacle.

Budget Tips Micro Wedding Off-Season Vendor Bundles

Cutting your wedding budget does not mean cutting your wedding's beauty, joy, or memorability. It means making smarter decisions about where each rupee generates the most experience per spend. Here are the highest-impact, lowest-regret savings strategies for Indian weddings in 2026.

→
Reduce guest count, not experience quality
Every guest removed from your list reduces catering, venue, invitation, and return gift costs simultaneously. Cutting from 400 to 300 guests on a ₹1,200/plate menu saves ₹1.2 lakh in catering alone — before venue savings. A wedding of 150 deeply loved guests is infinitely more joyful than 400 obligatory ones.
→
Choose an off-peak date or time slot
Weekday weddings, morning slots, and dates outside the November–February and April–May peak seasons can reduce venue costs by 30–45%. Your guests will adjust — the food, décor, and love are the same regardless of what day it falls on.
→
Bundle vendors for package discounts
Many photography studios, décor companies, and entertainment agencies offer 10–20% discounts when you book multiple services together (wedding + pre-wedding shoot, or décor for 3 events). Ask every vendor explicitly: "What do you offer if I book you for multiple events?"
→
Use seasonal and local flowers for décor
Out-of-season and imported flowers (orchids, peonies, garden roses in summer) cost 3–5x more than seasonal alternatives. Work with your décor team to design around what's naturally in abundance during your wedding month. Marigolds, tuberose, and seasonal greens can create breathtaking mandaps at a fraction of the cost.
→
Invest in digital invitations for most guests
Premium printed invitations at ₹500–2,000 per set add up quickly across 400 families. In 2026, beautifully designed digital invitations via WhatsApp or email are widely accepted for all but the closest family. Reserve physical invitations for parents, grandparents, and your 30–40 most cherished relationships.
→
Consolidate your ceremony events
Four-day wedding celebrations are a significant financial stretch for most budgets. A beautifully designed two-day wedding — mehendi/haldi on day one, ceremony and reception on day two — concentrates your budget, reduces vendor fatigue, and often creates a more emotionally resonant experience. Quality over duration.
💡

The 48-Hour Rule: When a vendor proposes an upgrade or addition during planning, wait 48 hours before agreeing. Most in-the-moment upgrades feel less essential after reflection, and many are forgotten entirely. This one discipline can save ₹1–3 lakh across the planning period.

Do You Need a Wedding Planner?

Planning & Coordination

Wedding Planner Coordination On-Day Management Day-of Coordinator

A wedding planner is one of the most debated budget items in Indian wedding planning. Critics argue it's an unnecessary luxury; advocates argue it saves more money than it costs. In 2026, the answer is nuanced — it depends entirely on the type of planner you hire and how you use them.

A full-service wedding planner takes over all vendor sourcing, negotiation, design, coordination, and on-day management for a fee that typically runs 8–12% of your total wedding budget. For a ₹30 lakh wedding, that's ₹2.4L – ₹3.6L. The case for this investment: experienced planners have vendor relationships that unlock negotiated discounts, they prevent expensive mistakes born of inexperience, and they allow you and your families to actually enjoy your own wedding instead of managing logistics.

A more budget-conscious option is a day-of coordinator — a professional who manages only the execution of your wedding day (vendor arrivals, timeline, problem-solving) after you've done all the planning yourself. Day-of coordinators charge ₹25,000 – ₹80,000 depending on city and scope, and for most couples they represent the highest-value professional hire in the entire wedding budget.

The Minimum Viable Planner: If a full-service planner is out of budget, hiring even a day-of coordinator is non-negotiable for your wellbeing on the wedding day. You will not be able to manage vendor arrivals, troubleshoot issues, and be emotionally present for your own wedding simultaneously. This is the one hire that pays for itself in peace of mind alone.

The Budget Payment Timeline

When Payments Fall Due

Payment Schedule Advance Booking Cash Flow Deposits

Understanding when money flows out is as important as understanding how much flows out. Indian wedding vendors typically operate on a structured advance-and-balance payment system. Misunderstanding this timeline has left many couples scrambling for liquidity at the wrong moment.

12M
12 Months Before — Venue Booking
Venue deposits: 30–50% of total venue cost. In metro cities during peak season, premium venues are booked 12–18 months in advance. This is often the largest single advance payment in the entire wedding budget.
9M
9 Months Before — Photographer & Key Vendors
Photography advance: 25–40% of package. Wedding planner advance (if applicable): 30–50%. In-demand vendors at this tier book 9–12 months out. Missing this window often means settling for your second choice.
6M
6 Months Before — Outfits & Décor
Bridal outfit advance to boutique or designer: 25–50%. Décor company retainer: 20–30%. Custom lehengas require 6–9 months for embroidery work — rush fees are significant and often involve compromising on quality.
3M
3 Months Before — Catering & Entertainment
Caterer confirmation and advance: 20–30%. DJ / band advance: 25–50%. Invitation printing or digital design finalisation. At this stage, 60–70% of your total budget should already be committed.
D-Day
Wedding Day & Week — Final Balances
Most vendors require balance payments on or before the wedding day. Prepare a cash/bank transfer schedule for the week of the wedding. Keep this day-of payment fund completely liquid — no investments, no term deposits.
⚠️

Never Pay 100% Upfront: No reputable vendor requires 100% payment in advance. If a vendor insists on full payment before service delivery, treat this as a significant red flag. Standard practice is 30–50% advance, with the balance on or after the day of service. Always get receipts for every payment made.
